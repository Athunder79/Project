Good Winter

Bon Iver is an indie folk band that originated from Eau Claire, Wisconsin in the United States. The band's name, "Bon Iver," is a play on the French phrase "bon hiver," which means "good winter."

The group was founded in 2007 by singer-songwriter Justin Vernon, who had previously been a member of the band DeYarmond Edison. After the break-up of that group, Vernon retreated to a remote cabin in Wisconsin where he spent three months recording his debut album, "For Emma, Forever Ago." The album was self-released in 2007 and gained critical acclaim for its raw, emotionally charged folk sound.

With the success of "For Emma, Forever Ago," Vernon assembled a band to perform live shows and record a follow-up album. The band's second album, "Bon Iver, Bon Iver," was released in 2011 and won the Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album. The album showcased the band's evolution towards a more expansive sound, incorporating elements of jazz and electronic music.

Since then, Bon Iver has released several more albums, including "22, A Million" (2016) and "i,i" (2019). The band's music has continued to evolve and experiment with new sounds and styles.

Bon Iver has received widespread critical acclaim for their music, with many praising Vernon's powerful vocals and the band's unique sound. The group has also collaborated with a variety of other artists, including Kanye West, James Blake, and Taylor Swift.
